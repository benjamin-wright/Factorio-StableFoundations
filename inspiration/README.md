# Stable Foundations

Ever wanted placeable tiles to provide more than just walkspeed? Aren't buildings supposed to be stronger and better with a more stable foundation? This mod reinforces structures and units when built over constructed tiles! Productivity, speed and efficiency are also improved for most assembly-type buildings.

![Damage](https://i.imgur.com/wLL5wVq.gif "Damage reduction based on foundation") ![Placement](https://i.imgur.com/pwGTRwg.gif "Text indicating foundation bonuses")

### Default Tile Bonuses: 
* **Stone Brick:**  15% damage reduction, +2% productivity, +4% speed, +5% efficiency *(Tier 1)*
* **Concrete:** 20% damage reduction, +3% productivity, +7% speed, +10% efficiency *(Tier 2)*
* **Refined Concrete:** 25% damage reduction, +5% productivity, +10% speed, +15% efficiency *(Tier 3)*

*(Note that mostly only intermediate products are eligible for productivity bonuses)*

#####
These apply to most destructible player-built structures on the mentioned tiles (damage reduction applies after initial resistances).  You are free to adjust damage reduction and productivity bonuses in the mod settings for more **balanced** or **casual** gameplay.

## Features:
* Increase assembly-type building productivity, efficiency and speed with foundations
* Configurable percent and flat damage reduction for stone, concrete and refined concrete tiles (tier-based system)
* Space Age compatible (gain extra bonuses for higher quality buildings on foundations)
* Whitelist tiles to the tier of your choice, as well as what buildings are affected by tile bonuses
* Filter certain types of buildings which can be eligible for damage reduction
* Adjusting friendly fire effectiveness with structures on tiles
* Reinforce things other than buildings, such as players and vehicles
* More configurable settings for technical players

## Mod Support/Suggestions:
* Language support is currently English, German and Simplified Chinese. Contributions are welcome!
* Most modded tiles may not be initially supported, feel free to request additions to default compatibility under discussions **(currently supports Asphalt Roads, KR2, IR3, Dectorio)**. If you have your own tile sets, you can also add them manually in the mod config via the *Tier x whitelisted foundations (tile)* setting.
